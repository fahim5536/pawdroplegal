Section 01

Data We Collect

When you interact with PawDrop — whether placing an order, browsing our store, or reaching out to us — we may collect the following categories of personal information:

Information You Provide Directly

  • Full name, email address, phone number, and billing/shipping address
  • Payment details (processed securely by third-party payment processors — we do not store full card data)
  • Account credentials if you create an account on our platform
  • Messages, reviews, and support communications you send us

Information Collected Automatically

  • IP address, browser type, operating system, and device identifiers
  • Pages visited, referral URLs, time on site, and clickstream data
  • Cookie identifiers and advertising tracking pixels (see Section 5)
  • Approximate geographic location derived from IP address

Information from Third Parties

  • Social media profile data if you sign in via Facebook, Google, or similar services
  • Shipping carrier updates from our fulfillment and logistics partners
  • Aggregated analytics data from advertising platforms (e.g., Meta Pixel, Google Ads)
Section 02

How We Use Your Data

We process your personal data only for legitimate, specified purposes. The legal bases for processing under GDPR are noted where applicable.

  • Order Fulfilment (Contract): Processing your purchase, coordinating with suppliers, arranging delivery, and sending shipping notifications.
  • Customer Support (Contract / Legitimate Interest): Responding to enquiries, resolving disputes, and managing returns or refunds.
  • Marketing Communications (Consent): Sending promotional emails, offers, or product updates — only where you have opted in. You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Personalisation (Legitimate Interest): Tailoring product recommendations, homepage content, and advertising based on browsing behaviour.
  • Analytics & Improvement (Legitimate Interest): Understanding how customers use our store to improve performance, layout, and product selection.
  • Fraud Prevention & Security (Legitimate Interest / Legal Obligation): Detecting suspicious transactions and protecting customer accounts.
  • Legal Compliance (Legal Obligation): Meeting tax reporting, accounting, and consumer protection obligations applicable in your jurisdiction.

We do not sell your personal data. PawDrop does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their own marketing purposes.

Section 03

Third-Party Sharing & Dropshipping

As an international dropshipping business, fulfilment of your orders requires us to share certain personal data with trusted third-party partners. We share only the minimum data necessary for each partner's specific role.

Fulfilment & Dropshipping Suppliers

Your name, shipping address, and order details are shared with our product suppliers and fulfilment partners (which may include overseas warehouses and logistics companies) solely to process and deliver your order. These partners are contractually prohibited from using your data for any other purpose.

Shipping & Logistics Carriers

Delivery partners (such as DHL, FedEx, USPS, Royal Mail, Australia Post, and Canada Post, among others) receive your name and delivery address to facilitate shipment.

Payment Processors

Payment is handled by PCI-DSS compliant processors (e.g., Stripe, PayPal). PawDrop does not have access to or store your full payment card details.

Marketing & Analytics Platforms

  • Meta Platforms (Facebook / Instagram Ads) — subject to Meta's own Data Policy
  • Google LLC — Google Analytics and Google Ads, subject to Google's Privacy Policy
  • Email marketing providers (e.g., Klaviyo, Mailchimp) for campaign delivery

Legal & Regulatory Authorities

We may disclose personal data to government authorities, law enforcement, or courts where legally required to do so, or where necessary to protect the rights, safety, or property of PawDrop or its customers.

All third-party data processors are bound by Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) and are required to implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures.

Section 04

International Data Transfers

PawDrop operates globally, and your personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your country of residence — including the United States, countries within the European Economic Area (EEA), and third countries where our suppliers or service providers are located.

Where personal data is transferred outside the EEA or UK, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
  • UK International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTAs) for transfers from the UK
  • Adequacy Decisions where the European Commission or UK government has determined the destination country provides adequate protection
  • Binding Corporate Rules or other recognised safeguards where applicable

You may request a copy of the specific transfer mechanisms applied to your data by contacting us at support@pawdrop.com.

Section 05

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies, pixels, and similar tracking technologies on our website to enhance your experience and support our marketing efforts. Cookies are small data files stored on your device.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Strictly Necessary: Required for the website to function (e.g., shopping cart, login sessions). These cannot be disabled.
  • Performance / Analytics: Help us understand site usage (e.g., Google Analytics). Enabled only with your consent.
  • Functional: Remember your preferences such as language, currency, or saved items.
  • Targeting / Advertising: Used to deliver relevant ads on platforms such as Facebook and Google. Enabled only with your consent.

You can manage your cookie preferences via our Cookie Consent banner on your first visit, or at any time through your browser settings. Note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our store.

Section 06

Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this policy, or as required by applicable law.

  • Order & Transaction Records: Retained for up to 7 years to satisfy tax, accounting, and commercial law obligations.
  • Customer Account Data: Retained while your account is active, plus 2 years after last activity, unless deletion is requested earlier.
  • Marketing Data: Retained until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent, plus 12 months thereafter for record-keeping.
  • Support Communications: Retained for up to 3 years from last interaction.
  • Analytics Data: Retained in anonymised or aggregated form; personally identifiable data deleted after 26 months.

Upon expiry of the applicable retention period, data is securely deleted or irreversibly anonymised.

Section 07

Data Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data from unauthorised access, loss, alteration, or disclosure. These include:

  • TLS/SSL encryption for all data transmitted between your browser and our servers
  • Strict access controls limiting data access to authorised personnel only
  • PCI-DSS compliant payment processing — we never store raw card data
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Incident response procedures in accordance with applicable breach notification laws

In the event of a personal data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours (as required under GDPR) and will inform affected individuals without undue delay where required.

Section 08

Your Rights

Depending on your country of residence, you have the following rights regarding your personal data. We will respond to all verified requests within 30 days.

📋
Right of Access
Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you. (GDPR Art. 15 / CCPA)
✏️
Right to Rectification
Request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete data. (GDPR Art. 16)
🗑️
Right to Erasure
Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention obligations. (GDPR Art. 17 / CCPA)
⏸️
Right to Restrict Processing
Request that we pause processing of your data in certain circumstances. (GDPR Art. 18)
📦
Right to Portability
Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format. (GDPR Art. 20)
🚫
Right to Object
Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing. (GDPR Art. 21)
↩️
Withdraw Consent
Withdraw any previously given consent for processing at any time, without affecting prior processing.
🏛️
Right to Complain
Lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority at any time.

California Residents (CCPA): You additionally have the right to know whether your personal information is being sold (it is not), and the right to opt out of any such sale. PawDrop does not discriminate against customers who exercise their privacy rights.

Supervisory Authorities: EU residents may contact their national Data Protection Authority (DPA). UK residents may contact the ICO (ico.org.uk). Australian residents may contact the OAIC (oaic.gov.au). Canadian residents may contact the OPC (priv.gc.ca).

To exercise any of the above rights, please contact us using the details in Section 11. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

Section 09

Children's Privacy

PawDrop's services are not directed to children under the age of 16 (or the applicable age of digital consent in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child, please contact us immediately at support@pawdrop.com and we will take prompt steps to delete such data.

When we make material changes, we will update the Effective Date at the top of this page and, where required by law, notify you by email or through a prominent notice on our website.

Your continued use of PawDrop's services after any update constitutes your acknowledgment of the revised policy. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
